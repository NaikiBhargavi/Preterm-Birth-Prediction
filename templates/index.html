PreTerm Birth

What is preterm birth?

Preterm birth refers to the delivery of a baby before 37 weeks of pregnancy, which is shorter than the typical 40-week gestation period. It can occur due to various factors, including maternal health issues, multiple pregnancies, or complications with the placenta. Babies born prematurely often face increased health risks, as their organs may not be fully developed. Depending on how early they are born, preterm infants may require specialized medical careto support their growth and development.
